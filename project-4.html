Coordinate Regression Advantages: Fast training and forward speeds, end-to-end full differential training. Disadvantages: Lacks spatial generalization ability. Explanation: Spatial generalization refers to the ability of a model to apply knowledge gained at one location during training to another location during inference. For instance, if a ball is always in the top left corner of the image during training, but in the bottom right corner during testing, the ability of the network to detect or recognize it signifies spatial generalization. Fully convolutional models possess this ability due to weight sharing. However, as the 2014 Network in Network paper pointed out, using fully connected layers to output coordinates greatly impairs spatial generalization.

Heatmap Regression Method: Typically involves generating a Gaussian heatmap at keypoint locations, creating a 2D Gaussian distribution centered at these points. This Gaussian distribution is then overlaid onto the corresponding location on the image to produce the final heatmap. A suitable size is chosen for the Gaussian kernel, which is then convolved with the original heatmap to smooth it. Advantages: High precision, improved spatial generalization ability. Disadvantages: Large output size leads to higher memory usage and slower inference and training speeds; theoretical lower bound of error; mse_loss might cause bias in learned results; not a fully differential model. Issue: Theoretical lower bound of error arises from the resizing process. For instance, if a keypoint at 507x507 in a 512x512 image is downscaled by a factor of 4, even an error-free Gaussian heatmap restoration would result in a maximum error of 3 pixels.
